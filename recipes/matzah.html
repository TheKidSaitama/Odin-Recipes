Matzah Recipe

How to make this Jewish classic

Ingrediants:

  1. 1 teaspoon all-purpose flour for dusting*
  2. 1 cup all-purpose flour
  3. >⅓ cup water, or more if needed
  4. 1 teaspoon olive oil, or as needed (Optional)
  5. ½ teaspoon kosher salt, or as needed (Optional)

Directions

  1. Step 1: Preheat the oven to 475 degrees F (245 degrees C). Move an oven rack to the top position. Preheat a heavy baking sheet in the oven.
  2. Step 2: Dust a clean work surface and a rolling pin with 1 teaspoon flour, or as needed.
  3. Step 3: Place 1 cup flour into a mixing bowl; set a timer for 16 minutes (18 minutes maximum). Start the timer; pour water, 1 tablespoon at a time, into flour. Stir with a fork until dough forms a rough ball. Transfer dough to the prepared work surface; knead rapidly and firmly until smooth, 30 seconds to 1 minute.
  4. Step 4: Divide dough into 4 equal pieces; cut each piece in half to get 8 pieces. Swiftly roll each piece into a ball. Working from the center, roll out each dough piece into a 5-inch-diameter round, dusting with flour as needed. Continue to gradually roll dough out to a diameter of about 8 inches, increasing each size by about 1 inch, then letting dough rest for a few seconds before rolling again to the finished size. The dough rounds will be very thin.
  5. Step 5: Use a fork to quickly pierce through each dough round about 25 times, all over, to prevent rising. Flip dough over and pierce each piece again 25 times.
  6. Step 6: With at least 5 minutes left on the timer, remove the hot baking sheet from the preheated oven and place dough rounds onto the baking sheet.
  7. Step 7: Bake on the top rack in the preheated oven for 2 minutes; flip matzah over and bake until lightly browned and crisp, about 2 more minutes.
  8. Step 8: Transfer to a wire rack to cool. Use a brush to lightly anoint each matzah with olive oil. Season generously with salt.
